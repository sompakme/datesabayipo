Huell Howser is an American voice artist, comedian, actor and TV personality who is best recognized as the host of California's Gold. He has an estimated net worth of $1.5 million. The show highlights numerous places of interest, events, small towns and landmarks in California which are not very known to the public. He has served as the producer for some of the show's specials.

Huell Burnley Howser was born on October 18, 1945 in Gallatin, Tennessee, USA. He attended University of Tennessee, Knoxville, where he completed his degree in history. He died of prostate cancer on January 7, 2013, at the age of 67.

Prior to his career on TV, Howser worked for the U.S. Marine Corps. He has also worked as one of the staff of Howard Baker who served as a US senator. He debuted on TV at the  WSM-TV. Eventually, he started working for  "Real Life" of WCBS-TV', then to KCBS-TV as a reporter. He then spent a year with Entertainment Tonight  as a correspondent and weekend host.
