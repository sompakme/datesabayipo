Celebrities Wearing Matching Holiday, Christmas Pajamas: Pics

’Tis the season for a pair of festive pajamas! To get into the holiday spirit, the stars are posing with their family and friends in adorable loungewear — and, lucky for Us, they’ve shared the snapshots.

From Kristen Bell in candy cane stripes to Hilary Duff in wrapping paper plaid, these sweet photos will have you feeling all warm and fuzzy whether you’re celebrating the 12 days of Christmas or eight nights of Hanukkah.

Just try keeping up with the Kardashians’ joyous PJ pics. Santa Claus came early for Kourtney Kardashian in 2018 when she showed off her reindeer set on Instagram with the caption, “Merry morning.” One year prior, her little sister Kylie Jenner posed in a ruby red top with white piping in 2017, nearly a year after giving birth to her first child, daughter Stormi, whom she shares with boyfriend Travis Scott.

Three years prior, Khloé Kardashian and her niece Penelope Disick celebrated the holidays in style by wearing matching reindeer-printed loungewear. “Auntie KoKo + P,” she captioned the cute Instagram shot.

Beverley Mitchell also got a jumpstart on the season, debuting her “holiday jammies” in November 2021 alongside her little ones. Despite being a full month ahead of the festivities, the 7th Heaven alum joked via Instagram, “P.S. don’t get the wrong impression, I am soooo not ready!!!!”

While jolly ol’ Saint Nick was busy making his list and checking it twice, Jessica Alba revealed she had an “obsession with coordinated pajamas.” In 2013, she rocked a pair of brightly colored footie pajamas for a pic with her husband, Cash Warren, and bestie Jaime King, who channeled Mrs. Claus with her own look. “Merry Christmas from the nerds in pj’s!” the Hart of Dixie alum wrote via Instagram.

However, it wasn’t the last time Alba would go all out with her seasonal sleepwear. In 2017, the Sin City star shared an image of her and her then-baby boy, Hayes, in their elf onesies.

The one-piece look was also popular with Julianne Hough and her brother, Derek Hough, who wore their black and red plaid get-ups that same year. Their dogs even joined in on the fun by wearing similar looks.
