The Highest-Grossing Gillian Anderson Movies, Ranked

  • The Spy Who Dumped Me - 2018 ($78.9 Million)
  • Johnny English Reborn - 2011 ($164.6 Million)

Hold on to your hats, folks! Today, we're diving deep into the fabulous filmography of the one and only Gillian Anderson. From aliens to detectives, and everything in between, this talented actress has graced the screen in a wide range of roles throughout her illustrious career. With her magnetic presence and commanding performances, Anderson has become a beloved icon of both the big and small screen. So, grab your popcorn, settle in, and get ready to explore the top 10 Gillian Anderson movies of all time. From thrilling sci-fi adventures to heart-wrenching dramas, this list has got it all. So, let's not waste any more time and dive right in!

Shadow Dancer - 2013 ($1.7 Million)

Via Flickr/Motion Masters

In Shadow Dancer, Anderson brought her acting chops to the role of Kate Fletcher, a slick and savvy MI5 agent who is tasked with recruiting an IRA informant. With co-star Clive Owen in tow, Anderson commands the screen with her portrayal of a determined and fearless agent, deftly maneuvering through the dangerous world of espionage. The film was a hit with audiences worldwide, bringing in a cool $1.8 million at the box office, cementing Anderson's reputation as a formidable leading lady in the spy-thriller genre.

Tristram Shandy: A Cock and Bull Story - 2006 ($3 Million)

Via Wikimedia Commons

Anderson had a role to die for in this meta-fictional comedy, playing the tantalizingly named Widow Wadman who threatens to steal the show. But, luckily for her co-stars Steve Coogan and Rob Brydon, Anderson's performance didn't overshadow the film's overall performance at the box office as it managed to rake in just over $3 million worldwide. Fans of witty and irreverent humor will definitely want to check this one out!

Crooked House - 2017 ($3.2 Million)

Via Flickr/English Riviera

In this Agatha Christie adaptation, Anderson played the cunning and mysterious Magda Leonides, one of the prime suspects in a family murder case. The film, which also starred the legendary Glenn Close and Terence Stamp, was a thrilling whodunit that kept audiences guessing until the very end. Despite its modest box office performance, grossing just over $3 million worldwide, Crooked House was praised for its stellar cast and gripping plot.

The Mighty - 1998 ($6.1 Million)

Via Flickr/Vision Entertainment

The Mighty, a 1998 drama, is a heartwarming story of two young boys who form an unlikely friendship. Anderson plays Loretta Lee, the mother of Kevin, a disabled boy who forms a strong bond with Max, a troubled teenager played by Kieran Culkin. Despite not being a blockbuster hit, The Mighty still managed to gross over $6 million at the worldwide box office. It was praised for its honest portrayal of disability and its message of hope and friendship.

How to Lose Friends & Alienate People - 2008 ($12 Million)

Via Wikimedia Commons

In the rollicking comedy, How to Lose Friends & Alienate People, Anderson portrayed the razor-sharp Eleanor Johnson. The film, based on journalist Toby Young's memoir, chronicles the writer's misadventures as he tries to navigate the cutthroat world of high-profile journalism in New York City. Alongside Anderson, the film also starred the hilarious Simon Pegg and the charming Kirsten Dunst. Despite its title, the film managed to win over audiences and raked in over $12 million at the global box office.

The Last King of Scotland - 2006 ($49.1 Million)

Via Flickr/Plex

Anderson's performance as Sarah Merrit, a doctor who becomes embroiled in the brutal regime of Ugandan dictator Idi Amin, was one of the highlights of this critically acclaimed biographical drama. Starring opposite Forest Whitaker, Anderson brought depth and nuance to her portrayal of a woman torn between her loyalty to her profession and her growing horror at the atrocities being committed around her. The Last King of Scotland was a commercial success as well, grossing over $49 million worldwide.

Kokuriko-zaka Kara - 2013 ($49.7 Million)

Via Wikimedia Commons

Anderson lent her voice to the character of Hana Matsuzaki in the English dub of this acclaimed Japanese animated film. Directed by Goro Miyazaki, the son of legendary filmmaker Hayao Miyazaki, Kokuriko-zaka Kara tells the story of a young girl who helps save a historic building from demolition. The film was a commercial success, grossing almost $50 million worldwide.

The X-Files: I Want to Believe - 2008 ($68.1 Million)

Via Wikimedia Commons

The much-anticipated return of the iconic Special Agent Dana Scully, played by Anderson, in the second X-Files feature film, titled The X-Files: I Want to Believe did not disappoint. Anderson's chemistry with David Duchovny, who played Special Agent Fox Mulder, was electric, and their banter and sleuthing skills kept audiences at the edge of their seats. The movie was a smash hit, earning a staggering $68.1 million at the worldwide box office, proving that the truth is still out there and that Anderson's star power is undeniable.

The Spy Who Dumped Me - 2018 ($78.9 Million)

Via Wikimedia Commons

The Spy Who Dumped Me is an action-packed comedy that featured Anderson as Wendy, the sharp-witted head of a top-secret British intelligence organization. Alongside co-stars Mila Kunis and Kate McKinnon, Anderson's performance helped the film earn almost $79 million worldwide, making it a hit among audiences looking for a blend of humor and thrills on the big screen.

Johnny English Reborn - 2011 ($164.6 Million)

Via Wikimedia Commons

Anderson played Pamela Thornton, an MI7 agent who assists Johnny English in his mission to stop an assassination attempt on the Chinese Premier, in the action-comedy movie, Johnny English Reborn. The film, which grossed a whopping $164.6 million worldwide, proves that Anderson has not only the acting chops but also the comedic timing to keep audiences on the edge of their seats. Whether she's playing a serious MI5 agent or a hilariously witty spy, Anderson proves she can take on any role with ease and finesse.
